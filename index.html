02 · Investment Thesis
Structural Driver
Ethernet wins the AI cluster war. TAM grows from $25B (2024) to $45B+ (2027) as AI training requires low-latency Ethernet fabrics. Arista holds 19% share (IDC Q4 2025) — the only pure-play beneficiary.
Moat
EOS is Arista's unbreakable moat. Hyperscalers standardize entire networks on EOS — switching is prohibitively expensive. Renewal rates exceed 99%; zero meaningful EOS competitor exists.
Underappreciated Catalyst
The 1.6T forced refresh is a guaranteed revenue event. Every GPU cluster built on 800G in 2025 must be re-cabled for 1.6T in H2 2026. Consensus underprices this by 15–20% — worth $2–3B incremental revenue. (25%+ growth assumed for explicit 2–3 year forecast period only, consistent with AI 1.6T cycle duration.)
Balance Sheet
$10.7B cash and zero debt = optionality at scale. $8.49 net cash per share (1.26B shares). Fortress balance sheet justifies a premium multiple and absorbs any demand slowdown without dilution.

04 · Variant Perception (Alpha Thesis)
❌ Consensus View
The market prices ANET as a cyclical hardware vendor with dangerous customer concentration. NVDA is seen as the primary AI beneficiary; networking is a commodity secondary play.
⚠ Why Incomplete
The "commodity" framing ignores EOS — Arista's proprietary OS creates deep software stickiness with 99%+ renewal rates. Hyperscalers can't easily switch vendors once standardized on EOS.
⚡ Our Edge
The 800G→1.6T upgrade is not optional — it's forced by AI training physics. Every cluster built in 2025 needs re-cabling in 2026. Consensus underprices this $2–3B guaranteed revenue event by 15–20%.
Key Insight: ANET is a recurring-revenue software infrastructure company disguised as a hardware vendor. When this re-rating occurs, the stock prices to 40–45× forward P/E → $175–$210.
